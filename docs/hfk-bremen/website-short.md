# in the digital shadow: An Embodied Debrief

*in the digital shadow: An Embodied Debrief* rereads the production process of *umbra: In the Digital Shadow* through accumulated traces and remnants: files, schedules, budgets, chat histories, self-tracking records, and the residual pressure that remained after the project had ended.

The project combines thesis research and installation. Its method, *Embodied Debrief*, shifts attention away from finished outcome and toward accumulation, allocation, delegation, and overload. This brings hidden costs of creative production into view: coordination labor, infrastructural friction, distributed agency, and recovery.

The installation translates this logic into a visitor-facing environment. The Collection Chamber captures traces and introduces them into the system's memory, while the Allocate Station links gesture, paper, and red-line marking to additional memory allocation. Across these elements, the work stages project memory as unstable, accumulating, and partially overloaded.

The research frame is organized through four modes: `Collect`, `Allocate`, `Delegate`, and `Overload`. Together they make it possible to read one project across accumulation, distribution, externalization, and limit conditions. Figures and reduced public datasets in the accompanying repository document how project mass, budget rhythm, delegated work, and embodied strain were reconstructed from mixed sources.
